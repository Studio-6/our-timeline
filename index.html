Coinlab was the first venture capital backed Bitcoin Company. In 2016, it patented technology intended to deanonymize bitcoin wallets and transactions. Established in 2011 by Peter Vessness and Mike Koss, Coinlab aimed to provide Mt.Gox, the largest bitcoin exchange at the time, with financial and investment partnerships in the US.
The Bitcoin Foundation was a non-profit organization founded in 2012 by a group of individuals including Peter Vessenes. Its mission was to standardize, protect, and promote the use of Bitcoin for the benefit of users worldwide. The foundation sought to educate policymakers and the public about the benefits of Bitcoin, worked to develop and promote technical standards, and provided financial and legal support to the development of open-source Bitcoin software.
The Satta Glove is a new wearable interface founded by Jordan Satta that aims to bring control back to performers. It allows musicians, dancers, and other performers to control their digital software with gesture and movement. The Satta Glove debut was featured on Wired, Yahoo! Tech, Output, and YesTheory. The company also established key partnerships with Sam Ash Music Stores and Hardware Accelerators to further support its development and distribution.
Inprov AI was an artificial intelligence music software engine beta that allowed musicians and composers to easily create new and unique music. It works by first translating MIDI data, which is a standardized format for digital music, into a style model. The Inprov AI engine then uses this style model as a basis to compose original music that is influenced by the input style. It enables user to generate new tracks with similar characteristics of the input making it easy to create orignal music that aligns with a certain style and taste with the help of AI.
NoteWorthy is a company that produces physical bitcoin banknotes, which are tangible versions of the digital currency bitcoin. These notes are denominated in bitcoin and can be exchanged like cash. They have a private key or QR code on them which can redeem the equivalent cryptocurrency value. They are useful in everyday transactions, gifting, and situations where digital infrastructure is limited.
Lamina1 is a layer one blockchain for the open metaverse founded by Peter Vessenes and Neal Stephenson. It aims to provide infrastructure for decentralized virtual worlds, enabling secure and transparent ownership, control and trade of virtual assets. It's blockchain technology promise to provide a secure, low-latency and scalable platform for next-generation virtual reality and metaverse experiences.
